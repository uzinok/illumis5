How using CBD can help parents combat stress and anxiety

Last modified on Tuesday 2 November 2021

Advertisement Promotion

Sarah Lafarge

Editor, Netmums Reviews

If there's one thing we've learnt in the last year it's that looking after your mental health is vital.

Long days balancing work deadlines and home schooling stir-crazy kids has taken its toll on even the most stable individual, leaving many parents with high levels of stress and anxiety.

Rather than reaching for a glass of wine or a cigarette to calm nerves and ease stress, there's something else that could balance your mood and help you get a better night's sleep – CBD.

You might heard of it already, but maybe aren't EXACTLY sure what is.

CBD stands for cannabidiol, which as the name suggests is derived from the cannabis plant, and it can work with your body to calm its stress response.

CBD is thought to aid our endocannabinoid system, often described as a bridge between our mind and body, which helps to regulate functions such as sleep, immune response and pain.

It's popping up as an ingredient in everything from massage oil to face masks and has been clocking up rave reviews about its health benefits.

But CBD is not just a flash in the pan trend and could be a simple way for parents to ease stress and live a calmer, more focused life.

And, before you start worrying, CBD is non-psychotropic and WON'T get you high – it simply brings down stress levels and helps you feel calm – in a completely legal way. Read more about its health benefits here.
